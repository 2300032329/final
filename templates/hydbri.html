Hyderabadi Biryani Recipe

Hyderabadi Biryani

Ingredients:

  • 500g Basmati rice
  • 500g chicken or mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, finely sliced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons Biryani masala
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • 3 cups water for cooking rice
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions:

  1. Rinse and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil and ghee in a pan. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add the chicken or mutton pieces, tomatoes, and cook until the meat is tender.
  5. Add yogurt, Biryani masala, garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for 5-10 minutes.
  6. In a separate pot, bring 3 cups of water to a boil. Add the soaked rice, cook until 70% done, and drain the water.
  7. In a large pot, layer the cooked meat mixture and partially cooked rice. Sprinkle the mint leaves, coriander leaves, fried onions, and lemon juice.
  8. Cover the pot with a tight lid and cook on low heat (dum) for 25-30 minutes.
  9. Serve hot with raita or salad. Enjoy your Hyderabadi Biryani!

Chef's Tips:

  • Use long-grain basmati rice for the best texture and fragrance.
  • You can also add boiled eggs to the Biryani for extra richness.
  • If using mutton, cook it longer until it is fully tender.
  • For a smoky flavor, you can add a piece of charcoal during the final dum cooking.
